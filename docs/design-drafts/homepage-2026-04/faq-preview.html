Background reading · CA campaign-finance basics

Frequently asked questions.

California has its own contribution limits and disclosure rules — separate from federal races. This page covers the basics: what's allowed, what's required, and where this site's data comes from.

§ 01

Contribution limits

What's the most an individual can give to a CA gubernatorial candidate?

For the 2026 cycle: $36,400 per individual per election (primary and general count separately, so an individual can give up to $72,800 across both). The Fair Political Practices Commission (FPPC) updates these limits each cycle for inflation; the figures below reflect the 2025–26 schedule.

Independent expenditures — outside groups not coordinated with a campaign — have no contribution limit on what they raise or spend. That's where most "unlimited" money flows in CA races.

Are contributions tax-deductible?

No. Political contributions to candidates, PACs, and IE committees are not deductible as charitable contributions on either federal or California state income tax.

§ 02

Disclosure & deadlines

When do candidates have to file?

California candidate-controlled committees file semi-annual Form 460 reports (Jan 31 covering July–Dec, July 31 covering Jan–June), plus pre-election reports before primary and general elections, plus 24-hour reports for any contribution of $1,000+ received within 90 days of an election.

What's reported about each donor?

For contributions of $100 or more, committees must disclose donor name, address, occupation, and employer. Below $100, only the cumulative total is reported (donor identity is not). Anonymous contributions over $100 must be returned or escheated to the state.

How quickly does this site update after a filing?

CAL-ACCESS publishes filings within hours of submission; this site mirrors them on a nightly sync. Polling data refreshes from RealClearPolitics on the same cadence. Last sync time is shown in the status bar at the top of every page.

§ 03

Prohibited activity

Who can't contribute?

Foreign nationals (with limited green-card exceptions). California state contractors during contract negotiation. Anonymous contributions over $100 (must be refunded). Cash contributions over $100 (must be refunded; California requires checks, cards, or wire transfers above that threshold).

Can a candidate use campaign funds for personal use?

No. California prohibits "personal use" of campaign funds, defined as expenses that exist irrespective of the campaign (mortgage, groceries, vacations). The FPPC publishes a detailed list at fppc.ca.gov.

§ 04

Independent expenditures

What counts as an "independent expenditure"?

Any payment for a communication that expressly advocates the election or defeat of a candidate — made without coordination with that candidate. TV ads, mailers, digital ads, and phone-banking all count. The committee paying must register with the FPPC and report each transaction.

Why are IE committees so much bigger than candidate committees?

Because contributions to IE committees are unlimited. A donor capped at $36,400 to a candidate's own committee can give millions to an IE committee that supports or opposes that same candidate — provided there's no coordination. This is the Citizens United regime; California adopted matching state-law guidance in 2010.

§ 05

About this data

Where do the numbers come from?

Campaign finance: direct mirrors of CAL-ACCESS filings (RCPT_CD, EXP_CD, IE rows). Polling: RealClearPolitics' aggregated horse-race polls plus the Wikipedia poll-aggregator page. Candidates: manually curated from official Secretary of State filings. Source attribution is shown on every section's footer.

How is the polling average computed?

We follow RealClearPolitics: a 30-day trailing window of all public polls, averaged per-candidate, recomputed nightly. Polls past 30 days fall out; new polls drop in. We don't apply house-effect adjustments or recency weighting; the goal is a transparent, reproducible mean.

Why does the same donor appear on multiple candidates?

Donors can give to multiple campaigns, up to the per-candidate limit. The Top Donors page aggregates a single donor's contributions across all 24 candidates and shows a per-candidate split underneath the row. The aggregation is name-normalized client-side from the ca_top_donors view.

§ 06

Corrections & contact

I see a mistake. How do I report it?

Email data@politicalintegrity.us. Include the URL, what you expected, and what you saw. Most data issues trace back to CAL-ACCESS itself; if a candidate filed an amended report, it'll be picked up on the next nightly sync.

Can I download the raw data?

Yes. CSV exports are available on the Candidates and Top Donors pages. The full dataset is also queryable via our /api endpoint — docs at api.politicalintegrity.us. Source code for the polling aggregation lives in the public ca-gov-polling repo.
